How Different Are
Ink And Graphite?

A glorified birthday card with a Children's Book along with it.

By Jerry Zhu

Diversity

Paper Samuel had been taught all his life that the "other" people were mean.

For what reason?

Samuel never knew. The first meeting of penned-people and paper-people before
the invention of loose-leaf was a long time ago.

There was no particular reason, but penned-people always looked harsher than paper-people.
Perhaps that was just how they were drawn in the particular book.

Either way, something about that idea frightened our little paper-person.

Of course it's to be understood that, these paper-kin had never seen each other before.

They always served as the villains of paper-tales or story-time to scare little paper people.

After all, what better enemy than someone who can't be identified with?

When Paper Samuel was a little stick-figure.
His mother read him all sorts of paper stories.
Although he preferred the books the bigger stick-figures were reading.

They were always more fantastical for him.
He never was able to keep attention for long periods of time.
